Red Sea shipping routes disrupted
What is happening

Commercial shipping through the Red Sea has declined following repeated attacks and security incidents involving cargo vessels. Several major operators have rerouted traffic away from the region.

Why this matters

The Red Sea carries roughly one eighth of global seaborne trade. Rerouting adds cost, increases transit times, and introduces uncertainty into energy, food, and manufacturing supply chains.
