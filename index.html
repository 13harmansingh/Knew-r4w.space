Red Sea · Trade Developing
Red Sea shipping routes disrupted
What is happening

Several major shipping firms have reduced Red Sea transit following repeated security incidents involving cargo vessels.

Why this matters

The route handles ~12% of global trade. Rerouting adds cost, delays goods, and introduces systemic uncertainty.

What changed: Two additional carriers announced temporary suspension since your last visit.
Some incident reports remain unverified. Attribution is disputed.

The Red Sea connects Europe and Asia via the Suez Canal. Ships are rerouting around southern Africa, adding 7–12 days.
