Updated Overview of KNEW

Knowledge Network of Every Witness — a real-time, location-grounded, AI-powered news intelligence network.

1. What KNEW Is

KNEW is a next-generation global news intelligence system designed to restore trust, context, and clarity in information consumption. It operates as a real-time, map-driven interface where users can explore news by location, understand multiple perspectives, detect bias, and receive AI-validated summaries of events happening anywhere in the world.

2. The Problem We Solve

  • Information overload and fragmented sources
  • Low transparency and undisclosed media ownership
  • Ideological and regional bias that distorts situational awareness
  • Limited tools to verify and compare perspectives across borders

3. The KNEW Insight

News is geography. Stories are rooted in locations, then amplified through networks of publishers and social channels. By grounding journalism in coordinates and applying multi-layer AI analysis, KNEW creates a real-time global information map.

4. What’s New Since Submission (28/10/2025)

  • Hybrid News Ingestion: 4-tier API fallback + RSS ingestion + legal HTML fallback scrapers + publisher crawlers
  • Hybrid AI Engine: 8-processor pipeline (content extraction, bias, sentiment, fact-checking, ownership, credibility, community, aggregator) with LLM + HF + local fallbacks
  • Architecture: Redis TTL fulltext, distributed quota locks, WebSocket job updates, unified document model
  • Frontend UX: live fetch on article open, improved map UI, real-time bias badges, clearer filters

5. How KNEW Works (Short)

  1. Ingest: APIs, RSS, scrapers normalize articles into a single document model; dedupe by embeddings + hashes.
  2. Process: Each article runs through 8 processors in parallel; results are aggregated into a trust score.
  3. Map: Articles are geocoded and presented on an interactive world map by location and category.
  4. Explore: Users launch articles, get AI summaries, compare sources, bookmark and track events across time.

6. Why This Matters

KNEW reduces bias exposure, improves cross-border situational awareness, and provides transparent, explainable trust scores. Target users include journalists, NGOs, analysts, governments, and informed citizens.

9. Legal & Operational Notes

  • Scraping respects robots.txt and publisher TOS by default
  • Full-text content is stored only temporarily (TTL in Redis)
  • Summaries and AI outputs may be persistently stored as metadata (not raw HTML)
