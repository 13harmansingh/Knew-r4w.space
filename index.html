Red Sea · Global trade Developing
Red Sea shipping routes disrupted
What is happening

Multiple shipping firms have suspended Red Sea transit following repeated attacks near the Bab el-Mandeb strait.

Why this matters

The route carries ~12% of global trade. Rerouting raises costs, delays goods, and increases insurance premiums.

Reporting from Reuters, the BBC, and the Financial Times indicates insurers have raised war-risk premiums sharply. Several navies have increased patrols, but attribution for recent incidents remains disputed.

EU · Technology Stable
EU AI Act enters enforcement phase
What is happening

EU member states have begun enforcing AI transparency and risk classification requirements.

Why this matters

The act sets a global precedent likely to influence AI governance beyond Europe.

According to the European Commission, systems classified as high-risk must now meet documentation and oversight standards. Coverage from AP and the FT highlights concerns from U.S. firms about regulatory fragmentation.
